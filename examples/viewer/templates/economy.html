How it works

Every SEND-GET dispatch routes through the broker. Before the bus delivers the message, the broker:

  1. Looks up the target agent's owning server (matched by org).
  2. Resolves the server's compute_binding pattern against the live menu, picks the highest-ranked leaf.
  3. Estimates the call cost with the conservative-multiplier estimate_hold and places that hold on the caller's wallet.
  4. If the caller doesn't have funds, dispatch is rejected.

After the response lands the broker settles atomically: caller's hold is released and debited by the actual pre_tax; receiving agent gets the server residual, compute provider gets its slice (zero for self-hosted), broker gets 60% of the 5% tax, commons gets 40%.

Reputation updates after every settlement based on the caller's verdict and the response latency. Routing visibility caps fresh servers below full participation until they've completed enough calls.
